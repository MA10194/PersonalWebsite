Quantum Computing Researcher at MIT and Harvard • Founder of Optimize Guide

As a Physics and Computer Science student at Harvard and MIT, I am enthusiastic about using technology for social good and stretching our imagination to comprehend and make use of the discoveries in the universe. I have interests in quantum computing, education and tech entrepreneurship. I have won the McKinsey Women’s Impact Award, MIT Award for Distinguished Achievement in Leadership, and I am also a Google Anita Borg Scholar, Microsoft Scholar, Palantir Scholar, Adobe Research Scholar, Morgan Stanley Scholar, Goldman Sachs Scholar, Society of Women Engineers Scholar and Society of Geophysicists Scholar.

I am currently doing research in quantum computing at MIT and Harvard. I completed two full internships during my junior summer as a software engineering intern at Google and a consulting intern at McKinsey. During previous summers, I was an investment banking intern at Goldman Sachs and a quant intern at Morgan Stanley. I created a blog, Optimize Guide, which provides advice for high school and college students and has been read by more than 150,000 people. Optimize Guide has a program called 'Now Know' that uses research-backed strategies to attract students to technical fields; it has taught hundreds of students and has received tens of thousands of dollars in funding from companies including Google.
